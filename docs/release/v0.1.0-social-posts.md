## Hacker News (Show HN)

Title:
Show HN: NeuroBridge - open-source middleware to adapt AI output for ADHD, autism, dyslexia

First comment:
I built NeuroBridge because most AI UX still assumes one reading/processing style. The core idea is simple: keep your current LLM, but pass responses through a deterministic adaptation layer tuned to cognitive profiles.

Why not just prompt it? Prompt-only adaptation is useful, but in production it drifts across models and prompt templates, and it is hard to enforce consistently across all app surfaces. Middleware gives one policy surface and consistent behavior.

How it works:
- profile selection (explicit or quiz)
- transform pipeline (chunking, urgency/tone rewrite, literalization, number context, reorder)
- output adapters for markdown/html/plain/json/tts
- optional memory/feedback loop for per-user tuning

The project is early and not a clinical device. It is inspired by accessibility research and community feedback, and I am explicitly looking for critique on where adaptation helps vs harms readability for real users.

If you have ideas for evaluation benchmarks, failure modes, or better profile modeling, I would love your feedback.
