# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, including neurodivergent people, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This community celebrates different ways of thinking.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Respecting different communication styles and processing needs.
- Giving and gracefully receiving constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and users.
- Taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting, derogatory comments, personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.
- Dismissing someone's lived experience related to disability or neurodivergence.
- Calling accessibility accommodations "special treatment" in a belittling or exclusionary way.

Zero tolerance applies to repeated or severe violations.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct and will take fair, appropriate corrective action in response to behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include project email, social media, community chat spaces, and events.

## Reporting

Report Code of Conduct incidents by opening a private security advisory or by contacting maintainers through the project's security reporting path in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may use these community impact guidelines:

1. Correction: private, written warning for minor first-time behavior.
2. Warning: clear notice and requested behavior changes.
3. Temporary Ban: temporary suspension from interaction channels.
4. Permanent Ban: permanent removal for severe or repeated violations.
